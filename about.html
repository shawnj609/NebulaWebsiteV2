Organization Overview

Education and cultural work delivered through founder-led programs.

Nebula's operating model combines nonprofit education programming and public art production. Activities are primarily delivered by the founding team with volunteer support and board oversight, scaling in line with funding and documented program demand.

Primary Program Structure

  • Nebula Academy: structured instruction and classes.
  • Nebula Lab: supervised open-lab practice sessions.
  • Nebula Pop-Ups: public-facing temporary installations.
  • Supporting activities: fundraising + administration.

Operating Geography + Venues

Austin-centered delivery

Current education and operational emphasis is in the Austin, Texas area, using partner schools and community venues including libraries, studios, and maker spaces.

Space strategy

Early-year programming uses borrowed or partner spaces, with a long-term goal of a consistent Nebula-controlled location for recurring classes and labs.

Resource and Staffing Commitments (Years 1-3)

Year 1

Founder and volunteer model

No paid staff or stipends planned; startup/compliance costs covered through founder contributions and in-kind support.

Year 2

Role-based stipends as funding allows

Grant-seeking, donor development, and insurance expansion to match increased activity and event participation.

Year 3

Part-time compensation transition as feasible

Targeted compensation for instruction, operations, planning, and administration with stronger reporting and documentation capacity.

Funding Model Summary

Primary sources

Founder contributions, charitable donations, grants/sponsorships, and in-kind support (space, equipment access, volunteer time).

Cost offsets

Some mission-aligned sessions may include modest fees for consumables/materials, with waivers or scholarships when applicable.

Administration share

Narrative planning allocates approximately 5% of total time and expenses to fundraising/administrative support functions.

Support the Mission

Support helps expand equipment access, maintain safe instructional delivery, and sustain public art programming that feeds back into education. If you want to sponsor a workshop, fund scholarships, or support Pop-Up production, reach out directly.
