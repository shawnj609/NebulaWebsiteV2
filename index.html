Educational + Cultural Nonprofit

Creative technology education rooted in real public art practice.

Nebula organizes its nonprofit programs into three connected tracks: Nebula Academy (instruction), Nebula Lab (guided practice), and Nebula Pop-Ups (public-facing installations). Programs are founder-led with volunteer support, primarily in Austin, Texas and partner venues.

Current Operational Snapshot

  • 2026 (Year 1): two semester-based drone education programs through school partnerships.
  • Estimated Year 1 student reach: approximately 40 participants.
  • Primary delivery region: Austin, Texas area schools and community venues.
  • No personal-residence drone flight activity; outdoor sessions run in approved spaces.

Nebula Academy

Structured workshops, classes, and school-partner programs in drone design, projection mapping, coding, and lighting.

Audience: ages 11+ (track-dependent)

Nebula Lab

Scheduled supervised open-lab sessions for enrolled learners and alumni, focused on guided iteration and confidence building.

Planned launch: Year 3 (2028)

Nebula Pop-Ups

Temporary installations and event experiences that serve communities while generating practical lessons for curriculum and operations.

Year 1 target: 2 major public Pop-Ups

Past, Present, Planned

2023-2025

Pilot Phase Before Formal Operations

Academy activity centered on demonstrations and partner development, including an Akins High School drone demo, planning discussions with Texas School for the Deaf, and University of Texas drone class involvement.

2026

Year 1 Proof of Concept

Two semester-based school partnerships for drone education (~40 students), plus two major Pop-Ups: Burning Man and Flipside.

2027-2028

Scale with Capacity Controls

Year 2 goals include four one-day workshops and five semester partnerships (140-180 participants). Year 3 goals include 6-8 workshops/classes annually, 5+ semester partnerships, 150+ annual participants, and Nebula Lab launch.

Program Delivery

Founder-led instruction

Unless otherwise noted, activities are conducted by the founding team with volunteers. Expansion is tied to available funding and documented demand, with role-based compensation phased in as feasible.

Access-first model

Programs are designed for learners who may lack equipment, software, capable computers, space, or mentorship. Shared tools, supervised sessions, and structured guidance are core to reducing entry barriers.
