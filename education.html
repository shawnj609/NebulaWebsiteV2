Structured instruction

Nebula Academy

Semester programs, workshops, and multi-session classes that take learners from fundamentals to concrete creative outputs.

Core areas include drones, projection mapping, creative coding, show control, and digital lighting.

Best for youth, young adults, and adults who want guided teaching, step-by-step progression, and documented outcomes.

Supervised practice

Nebula Lab

An open-lab model for enrolled learners and alumni who need supported time with equipment, software, and iteration.

The focus is repetition, troubleshooting, confidence building, and practicing the same real systems introduced in Academy.

Planned as a Year 3 expansion once equipment access, staffing, and recurring space are in place.

Program Delivery

Founder-led instruction

Unless otherwise noted, activities are conducted by the founding team with volunteers. Expansion is tied to available funding and documented demand, with role-based compensation phased in as feasible.

Access-first model

Programs are designed for learners who may lack equipment, software, capable computers, space, or mentorship. Shared tools, supervised sessions, and structured guidance are core to reducing entry barriers.

Program locations

Austin, Texas region: partner schools, libraries, community centers, studios, maker spaces, and supervised outdoor test spaces.
