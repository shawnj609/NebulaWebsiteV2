Nebula Academy

Hands-on creative technology education with documented outcomes.

Nebula Academy is structured as semester programs, one-day workshops, and multi-session modules. Courses start with fundamentals and progress toward concrete outputs such as a supervised demonstration, mapped projection piece, or code-based show-control study.

Who It Serves

  • Primary audience: youth, young adults, and adults with limited access to tools and mentorship.
  • General Academy design target: approximately ages 11 and up.
  • Digital Lighting Design & Control track: adults 18+.
  • Beginner-friendly by default, expandable for learners with minimal prior experience.

Current Course Pathways

Each pathway combines instruction, demonstration, guided practice, and light documentation for participant portfolios.

Drone Show Design & Operation

Flight-path design, control software operation, drone preparation, previs simulation, supervised testing, and iteration. Typical delivery mixes indoor design sessions with supervised outdoor flight sessions.

Projection Design & Mapping

Projector setup, visual content development, and mapping across physical surfaces from simple to complex. Includes testing in controlled-light spaces and iterative refinement.

Creative Coding / Show Control (Project Flare)

Open-source learning through real show systems: timing, interactive logic, and spatial reasoning for projection, lighting, and synchronized media behavior.

Digital Lighting Design & Control

DMX foundations, pixel LED systems, Madrix workflows, controller interfaces (MIDI + hardware), WLED configuration, safety fundamentals, and Unreal Engine previsualization.

Typical Session Flow

1. Immediate feedback

Early sessions prioritize cause-and-effect: participants see tools respond quickly to build confidence.

2. Systems understanding

Instructors unpack data flow and physical setup: software, controllers, wiring, power, mapping, and safety practices.

3. Outcome + reflection

Learners complete a small output and document the process (photos/video/files) for internal review or portfolio use.

Scheduling pattern

Programs are commonly delivered weekly over multiple weeks. Digital Lighting sessions typically run 2-3 hours each. Enrollment is capped according to instructor bandwidth, available equipment, and safety constraints.
