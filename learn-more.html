Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

Academy offerings are designed for youth, young adults, and adults (roughly ages 11+), with track-specific constraints. Digital Lighting Design & Control is currently targeted to adults (18+).

How do people enroll?

Through direct sign-up opportunities and school/community partnerships. Enrollment is capped by safety, equipment, and instructor capacity.

Where do sessions happen?

Primarily Austin-area partner schools, community venues, studios, maker spaces, and approved outdoor sites for supervised flight activity when required.

What outcomes are expected?

Participants complete practical studies such as flight sequences, projection-mapped pieces, lighting scene files, or show-control demonstrations, with light documentation for reference/portfolio use.

Is this accessible for beginners?

Yes. Courses are beginner-friendly and structured for learners who may lack prior access to equipment, software, and technical mentorship.

Does Nebula run open lab access?

Nebula Lab is planned as a supervised open-practice model for enrolled learners and alumni, with launch targeted in Year 3 (2028).

Digital Lighting Design & Control: Quick Summary

  • Weekly, multi-week format with sessions typically 2-3 hours.
  • Hands-on DMX and pixel-based LED control workflows.
  • Madrix software, MIDI/hardware controllers, and WLED-based configuration.
  • Previsualization in Unreal Engine (visual planning focus).
  • Safety fundamentals: power, current, voltage, and heat practices.
