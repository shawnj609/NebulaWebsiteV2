Applied Learning Infrastructure

Accessible toolchains that connect software, hardware, and public art.

Nebula programs focus on systems participants can understand and use in practice. The goal is technical literacy, creative confidence, and safe deployment habits rather than black-box spectacle.

Core Principle

Learners should understand how data moves from software to physical output: pathing to drones, pixels to LEDs, media to projection surfaces, and timed cues to show-control systems.

Systems Used in Current Tracks

Drone design + supervised operation

  • Flight-path creation and control software orientation.
  • Previsualization and supervised flight testing in appropriate spaces.
  • Iteration loops with instructor feedback and documented outcomes.

Projection design + mapping

  • Projector setup and media alignment on physical surfaces.
  • Progression from found media to participant-created visual content.
  • Testing in controlled-light environments with practical troubleshooting.

Creative coding / show control (Project Flare)

  • Open-source instruction focused on logic, timing, and spatial behavior.
  • Code-based studies like timed visuals and interactive cue sequences.
  • Potential integration into Academy showcases and Pop-Up prototypes.

Digital lighting design + control

  • DMX concepts, pixel LED systems, and Madrix-based control workflows.
  • MIDI pads and hardware controller interfaces.
  • Firmware-oriented setup with WLED for configuration and mapping.
  • Unreal Engine previsualization for layout and timing experiments.

Safety + Scope Boundaries

Safety instruction is explicit

Lighting modules include power/current/voltage/heat best practices. Flight activity is supervised and venue-dependent.

No personal-residence flight operations

Outdoor drone sessions are conducted in approved spaces such as fields, lots, or private property with relevant permissions.

Beginner-accessible tools

Programs avoid requiring proprietary enterprise pipelines. Instruction prioritizes open or widely available systems where possible.

Why This Stack Matters

Many participants experience lighting, drone choreography, and mapped visuals only as finished experiences. Nebula's technology curriculum opens the system architecture itself: what signals are sent, how fixtures are configured, how timing is maintained, and where creative decisions are made. This directly supports the nonprofit's educational mission and makes public-art production knowledge reachable for learners who otherwise lack access to equipment and mentorship.

Technology + Curriculum

Technical methods are selected for teachability, safety, and community-scale deployment potential.
