Nebula Pop-Ups

Temporary installations that serve the public and strengthen instruction.

Pop-Ups are site-specific art experiences delivered in community and festival contexts. They are collaborative, project-lead driven, and intentionally temporary, allowing rapid experimentation while prioritizing safety, accessibility, and meaningful engagement.

How Pop-Ups Function

  • Each project has a designated lead for schedule, contributor coordination, and safety practice.
  • On-site execution is public-facing and participatory where context allows.
  • Post-event lessons feed directly into Academy and Lab curriculum updates.
  • Projects may be preserved, reused, or deconstructed for material repurposing.

Venue and Community Context

Regional events

Texas regional burns (including Flipside and Freezer Burn) and large-scale arts gatherings such as Burning Man are core contexts for Pop-Up deployment.

Austin-based installations

Independent local Pop-Ups in Austin event spaces support broader public access and right-sized experimentation.

Why Pop-Ups Matter for Education

Pop-Ups are the applied testing ground for new projection methods, show-control ideas, interaction concepts, and setup/safety workflows. This creates a practical feedback loop: what works in the field gets documented and folded back into Academy and Lab instruction, improving relevance for students and strengthening operational quality over time.

Public Art Programming

Pop-Ups are designed to be inclusive, collaborative, and culturally additive in the communities where they are presented.
